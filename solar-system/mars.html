Mars

Mars is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. There is strong evidence Mars was – billions of years ago – wetter and warmer, with a thicker atmosphere.

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun – a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. Mars is also a dynamic planet with seasons, polar ice caps, canyons, extinct volcanoes, and evidence that it was even more active in the past.

Mars, often referred to as the "Red Planet," is a captivating world that has captured human imagination for centuries. With its rusty-red hue and visible surface features, Mars has been a focal point for astronomers and space exploration missions. The planet's distinct appearance is due to the iron-rich dust and rocks that cover its surface, giving it a unique and iconic color.

One of the most intriguing aspects of Mars is the possibility of past or present life. Evidence suggests that Mars once had liquid water on its surface, and scientists continue to search for signs of microbial life in its ancient history. This quest has driven numerous missions, including rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance, which have analyzed Martian soil and rocks to uncover clues about the planet's potential habitability.

F A C T S A B O U T m a r s
1

Fourth Planet from the Sun

Mars is the fourth planet in our solar system, situated between Earth and Jupiter.

2

Distinct Red Color

Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its iron-rich surface, which gives it a reddish appearance when viewed from space.

3

Thin Atmosphere

Mars has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with traces of nitrogen and argon. This thin atmosphere contributes to its cold temperatures and lack of significant air pressure.

4

Olympus Mons

The largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, stands on Mars. It's about 13.6 miles (22 kilometers) high, making it nearly three times the height of Mount Everest.

5

Valles Marineris

Mars boasts Valles Marineris, a colossal canyon system stretching over 2,500 miles (4,000 kilometers) long and up to 7 miles (11 kilometers) deep.

6

Polar Ice Caps

Mars has ice caps at its poles, consisting of water ice and frozen carbon dioxide. These caps expand and contract with the changing seasons.

7

Curiosity Rover

NASA's Curiosity rover has been exploring Mars since 2012, investigating its geology, climate, and potential habitability. It confirmed the presence of ancient riverbeds and analyzed Martian rocks for clues about past conditions.

8

Potential for Liquid Water

Despite the planet's extreme cold, evidence suggests that liquid water might exist in subsurface reservoirs, raising intriguing questions about the possibility of microbial life.

9

Two Small Moons

Mars has two irregularly shaped moons, Phobos and Deimos, both believed to be captured asteroids. They are much smaller than Earth's moon.
