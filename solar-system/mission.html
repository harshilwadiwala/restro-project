Missions

Missions to explore our solar system have been at the forefront of human curiosity and scientific discovery. These missions, undertaken by agencies like NASA and ISRO, have unveiled the mysteries of distant planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. From the pioneering Voyager probes that ventured to the outer planets to ISRO's Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) and NASA's Mars rovers that have unveiled the Red Planet's secrets, these missions have expanded our understanding of planetary geology, atmospheres, and potential habitability.

Both NASA and ISRO have undertaken significant missions to explore the mysteries of our solar system. NASA, the United States' space agency, has conducted a diverse array of missions, including the iconic Voyager program that ventured into interstellar space, the Mars Exploration Program with its rovers uncovering the Martian landscape, and the groundbreaking New Horizons mission that unveiled Pluto's secrets. Additionally, NASA's missions such as Cassini-Huygens, Juno, and OSIRIS-REx have provided unprecedented insights into Saturn, Jupiter, and near-Earth asteroids.

On the other hand, ISRO, India's space agency, has made remarkable contributions with its missions. The Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) stands out as India's inaugural interplanetary endeavor, successfully entering Mars' orbit and conducting scientific observations. ISRO's Chandrayaan missions have illuminated the Moon's mysteries, with Chandrayaan-1's discovery of lunar water molecules and Chandrayaan-2's ambitious orbiter, lander, and rover ensemble enhancing lunar exploration efforts.

Both agencies continue to inspire the world with their missions, enriching our understanding of the solar system and advancing human knowledge in the realm of space exploration.

International space station

The International Space Station (ISS) is a collaborative orbital laboratory, home to astronauts from various countries, conducting scientific research and technological experiments in microgravity. It serves as a testament to international cooperation in space exploration, fostering advancements in diverse fields and laying the foundation for future space missions.

apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the historic NASA mission that achieved humanity's first moon landing on July 20, 1969, with astronauts Neil Armstrong and Edwin "Buzz" Aldrin becoming the first humans to set foot on the lunar surface. Armstrong's iconic words, "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind," marked a monumental moment in human exploration.

mangalyaan

Launched in 2013, Mangalyaan is India's first interplanetary mission. It successfully entered Mars' orbit and conducted scientific observations of the Martian surface, atmosphere, and mineral composition.

chandrayan

Chandrayaan-1 made significant discoveries, including confirming the presence of water molecules on the lunar surface.The spacecraft was orbiting around the Moon at a height of 100 km from the lunar surface for chemical, mineralogical and photo-geologic mapping of the Moon.

Mars rover

In January 2004, two robotic geologists named Spirit and Opportunity landed on opposite sides of the red planet.Carrying identical, sophisticated sets of science instruments, both rovers have found evidence of ancient Martian environments where intermittently wet and habitable conditions existed.

juno

Understand origin and evolution of Jupiter, look for solid planetary core, map magnetic field, measure water and ammonia in deep atmosphere, observe auroras. NASA's Juno mission contributes to our understanding of the origin and evolution of Earth by studying Jupiter's composition, magnetic field, and atmospheric dynamics.
