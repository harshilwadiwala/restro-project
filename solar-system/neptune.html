neptune

Neptune—the eighth and most distant major planet orbiting our Sun—is dark, cold and whipped by supersonic winds. It was the first planet located through mathematical calculations.

Distance From Sun

2,780,033,319 mi

One way Light Time to Sun

248.729369 mins

Length of Year

60,190 Earth Day

Planet Type

Ice Giant

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds, ice giant Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet in our solar system.

More than 30 times as far from the Sun as Earth, Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye and the first predicted by mathematics before its discovery. In 2011 Neptune completed its first 165-year orbit since its discovery in 1846.​

1

Unusual Tilted Axis

Uranus rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. This unique orientation likely resulted from a collision early in its history.

2

Cold Atmosphere

Uranus has an icy and frigid atmosphere, composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane. Its average temperature hovers around -224°C (-371°F), making it one of the coldest planets in our solar system.

3

Blue-Green Appearance

Uranus appears blue-green due to the presence of methane in its upper atmosphere. This coloration gives it a distinct visual appearance compared to other gas giants.

4

Rings and Moons

Uranus has a faint ring system and 27 known moons. The five largest moons are Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon.

5

Miranda's Unusual Terrain

Miranda, one of Uranus' moons, features a diverse and jumbled terrain with cliffs, valleys, and canyons that suggest a complex geological history.

6

Magnetic Tilt

The planet's magnetic field is also tilted, being offset from its rotational axis by about 60 degrees. This magnetic field interaction is another result of Uranus' unique rotation.

7

Sparse Sunlight

Due to its vast distance from the Sun, Uranus receives only about 1/400th of the sunlight that Earth receives. This contributes to its extremely cold temperatures.

8

Hydrogen and Helium Interior

Beneath its hydrogen and helium atmosphere, Uranus has an interior composed of a mixture of water, ammonia, and methane compounds. This "icy" interior is distinct from the rocky cores of Jupiter and Saturn.

9

named After a Sky God

Uranus is named after the Greek god of the sky, Ouranos. It was the first planet discovered using a telescope by William Herschel in 1781.
