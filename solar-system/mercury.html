Mercury

From the surface of Mercury, the Sun would appear more than three times as large as it does when viewed from Earth, and the sunlight would be as much as 11 times brighter.

Planet Type

Terrestrial

The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

From the surface of Mercury, the Sun would appear more than three times as large as it does when viewed from Earth, and the sunlight would be as much as seven times brighter. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet in our solar system – that title belongs to nearby Venus, thanks to its dense atmosphere.

Because of Mercury's elliptical – egg-shaped – orbit, and sluggish rotation, the Sun appears to rise briefly, set, and rise again from some parts of the planet's surface. The same thing happens in reverse at sunset.

F A C T S A B O U T m e r c u r y
1

Closest to the Sun

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun in our solar system, with an average distance of about 57.9 million kilometers (36 million miles).

2

Extreme Temperature Changes

Due to its lack of a significant atmosphere to retain heat, Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations, ranging from scorching daytime highs of about 430°C (800°F) to frigid nighttime lows of around -180°C (-290°F).

3

Slow Rotation

Mercury has a slow rotation, taking about 59 Earth days to complete one rotation on its axis. However, its day (one rotation) is longer than its year (orbit around the Sun), which takes about 88 Earth days.

4

Thin Atmosphere

Mercury's atmosphere is incredibly thin, composed mainly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen. It's not sufficient to support human life or to significantly affect weather patterns.

5

Impact Craters

The planet's surface is covered with impact craters due to its lack of significant geological activity. The largest of these craters is the Caloris Basin, which is about 1,550 kilometers (963 miles) in diameter.

6

Unusual Orbit

Mercury's orbit is eccentric, meaning it's not a perfect circle. This results in significant variations in its distance from the Sun as it travels around its elliptical path.

7

Weak Magnetic Field

Mercury has a weak global magnetic field, which is about 100 times weaker than Earth's. It's thought to be generated by a partially liquid iron core.

8

Metal-Rich Composition

Mercury's high density suggests that it has a large metal-rich core, making it one of the most metal-rich planets in the solar system .

9

Thin Exosphere

Mercury has a very thin exosphere, which consists of atoms that have escaped the planet's surface. These atoms include hydrogen, helium, oxygen, sodium, and potassium.
