Jupiter

Jupiter is more than twice as massive than the other planets of our solar system combined. The giant planet's Great Red Spot is a centuries-old storm bigger than Earth.

Gas Giant

Jupiter has a long history of surprising scientists – all the way back to 1610 when Galileo Galilei found the first moons beyond Earth. That discovery changed the way we see the universe.

Fifth in line from the Sun, Jupiter is, by far, the largest planet in the solar system – more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined.

Jupiter's familiar stripes and swirls are actually cold, windy clouds of ammonia and water, floating in an atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter’s iconic Great Red Spot is a giant storm bigger than Earth that has raged for hundreds of years.

F A C T S A B O U T j u p i t e r
1

Giant of the Solar System

Jupiter is the largest planet, with a diameter of about 139,822 kilometers (86,881 miles) – more than 11 times that of Earth.

2

Rapid Rotation

Jupiter has the shortest day of all the planets, completing one rotation on its axis in just about 9.9 hours. This rapid rotation causes the planet to bulge slightly at its equator.

3

Great Red Spot

Jupiter's iconic Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for centuries. It's so large that it could fit Earth within its boundaries.

4

Gas Giant

Jupiter is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. It lacks a solid surface, and its composition is more similar to that of a star than a rocky planet.

5

Strong Magnetic Field

Jupiter has an incredibly powerful magnetic field, about 20,000 times stronger than Earth's. This field creates intense radiation belts around the planet.

6

Moon King

Jupiter has the most moons of any planet in our solar system, with over 80 known moons. The four largest moons – Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto – are known as the Galilean moons.

7

Galilean Moons

The Galilean moons are fascinating worlds in their own right. For example, Europa is believed to have a subsurface ocean that might contain the conditions suitable for life.

8

Ring System

Despite the planet's extreme cold, evidence suggests that liquid water might exist in subsurface reservoirs, raising intriguing questions about the possibility of microbial life.

9

Strongest Storms

Jupiter experiences some of the strongest storms in the solar system, including lightning and thunderstorms. The planet's thick atmosphere is a chaotic mix of bands and belts of clouds.
